An Inside Joke Since 1973

See You Next Wednesday

Originally the title of a screenplay written by a teenage John Landis, the phrase has become cinema’s most enduring recurring Easter egg.

1973

Schlock

The first appearance, mentioned twice and featured on a movie poster in the background.

1977

The Kentucky Fried Movie

The title of the "Feel-a-Rama" movie being watched by the theater audience.

1980

The Blues Brothers

Visible on multiple billboards throughout the city of Chicago during the high-speed chase.

1981

An American Werewolf in London

The title of the fictional pornographic film playing in the theater where the transformation reaches its peak.

1983

Trading Places

A movie poster visible in Ophelia's apartment, reimagined as a classic drama.

1983

Michael Jackson's Thriller

Spoken by a deputy in the movie-within-the-video; also appears on a theater poster in the background.

1985

Spies Like Us

Featured on a U.S. Army recruiting poster in the background of a military base.

1988

Coming to America

A subway poster advertising a film starring the director's frequent collaborators, Jamie Lee Curtis and Dan Aykroyd.

1992

Innocent Blood

The title is prominently displayed on a movie theater marquee in a background shot.

1996

The Stupids

A clever background placement visible on the side of a passing city bus.

Originally inspired by 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
