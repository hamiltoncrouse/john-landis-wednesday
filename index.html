An Inside Joke Since 1973

See You Next Wednesday

Originally the title of a screenplay written by a teenage John Landis, the phrase has become cinema’s most enduring recurring Easter egg—a spectral signature appearing across decades of work.

1973

Schlock

The genesis. Mentioned twice and featured on a movie poster in the background of Landis' directorial debut.

 1977

The Kentucky Fried Movie

The title of the "Feel-a-Rama" movie being watched by the theater audience—a tactile cinematic experience.

 1980

The Blues Brothers

Woven into the urban fabric of Chicago; visible on multiple billboards during the high-speed pursuit of the divine mission.

 1981

An American Werewolf in London

The title of the fictional pornographic film playing in the Piccadilly Circus theater during the visceral climax.

 1983

Trading Places

A classic drama poster in Ophelia's apartment, subverting the expectations of the Landis universe.

 1983

Michael Jackson's Thriller

Spoken by a deputy in the movie-within-the-video; the line echoes through pop culture history.

 1985

Spies Like Us

Embedded in military bureaucracy on a U.S. Army recruiting poster in the background of the base.

 1988

Coming to America

A subway poster advertising a film starring frequent collaborators Jamie Lee Curtis and Dan Aykroyd.

 1992

Innocent Blood

Prominently displayed on a movie theater marquee, continuing the tradition into the nineties.

 1996

The Stupids

A fleeting background placement on the side of a passing city bus, a hidden reward for the observant viewer.
Originally inspired by 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
