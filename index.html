SEE YOU NEXT WEDNESDAY

The John Landis Easter Egg

Originated as the title of a screenplay written by a 16-year-old John Landis, this phrase has appeared in nearly every project he has directed since.

Schlock (1973) Mentioned twice and featured on a movie poster in the background.
The Kentucky Fried Movie (1977) The title of the "Feel-a-Rama" movie being watched.
The Blues Brothers (1980) Appears on multiple billboards throughout the city of Chicago.
An American Werewolf in London (1981) The title of the fictional pornographic film playing in the Piccadilly Circus theater.
Trading Places (1983) A movie poster in Ophelia's apartment.
Michael Jackson's Thriller (1983) Spoken by a deputy in the movie-within-the-video; also appears on a theater poster.
Spies Like Us (1985) Appears on a U.S. Army recruiting poster.
Coming to America (1988) A subway poster advertising the film starring Jamie Lee Curtis and Dan Aykroyd.
Innocent Blood (1992) Advertised on a movie theater marquee in the background.
The Stupids (1996) Visible on the side of a bus.
"See you next Wednesday" - Originally from 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
